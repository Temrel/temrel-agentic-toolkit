The Agentic Toolkit : evals and verification

Verification Starter

A copy-me verification layer for agent-written code. Deterministic gates catch the cheap mistakes, an LLM judge checks the diff against what it was supposed to do, and a small eval proves the judge catches your real failures. You review last, not first.

Everything on this page runs locally in your browser. The generator just writes a text file, nothing is uploaded.

The three tiers

Tier 1
Code-based graders
Typecheck, tests, lint, build. Deterministic, fast, and effectively free. Run them first, on every change, wired to a trigger so they fire without you.
Tier 2
Model-based grader
An LLM judge reads the diff against the stated intent, not just whether it builds. It catches silent no-ops, pretend implementations, deleted tests, and scope creep.
Tier 3
Human grader
You, reviewing only what survives tiers 1 and 2, with a report that says where to look first. Your eyes are the scarcest resource in the loop: spend them last.

Then, in your repo

  1. Grab the starter folder from the repo and copy it in, for example as verification/.
  2. Drop your generated verify.config.sh next to verify.sh.
  3. Run ./verify.sh gates for the deterministic gates, and ./verify.sh check --intent "what the change was meant to do" to judge the diff against intent.
  4. Record every agent PR that gets kicked back in review as a line in cases/cases.jsonl. At about 20 cases, ./verify.sh eval scores your judge with a number instead of a feeling.
  5. Wire it on a trigger (git pre-push hook, Claude Code hook, or a watch loop) so it fires without you. Examples ship in triggers/.
